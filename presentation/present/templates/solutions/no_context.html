This puzzle is a diagramless crossword, where all clues are presented in a single, undifferentiated list, with only a set of 10 context-free grammars mapping clues to grid entries. Solvers need to solve the clues and determine the mappings between clues and grid entries via the grammars.

Additionally, the grammars provide information to help fill out the grid. E.g. grammar S6 can be used to determine that the seven clues matching it are all 4 letter words starting with B, M, O, R, or T, helping to fill the grid even if the matching crossword clues have not been solved yet.

Once the grid has been solved, the final extraction is performed by finding locations where the clues matching a given grammar intersect themselves (one per grammar) and reading them out in S1–S10 order (which is also grid order). This gives the answer CHECKPOINT.
